# Design research: OutsourcedCompany.com

Date: 2026-07-21
Search intent: outsourcing company Philippines

## What the current site needed

The committed homepage was a generic staffing template. It had no competitor research, used a third-party hero URL, carried unsupported savings and launch-time figures, and still described the offer with broad terms such as "offshore staff." The thank-you page was also a placeholder. A clean working tree did not make it ready for handoff.

## Five direct competitors inspected

### 1. Sourcefit

URL: https://sourcefit.com/

The desktop homepage uses an oversized black-and-orange headline beside a facility video and a stack of benefit accordions. Its best idea is the process-first pitch: people matter, but the operating system around them matters too. Scale metrics and a detailed location footer add substance. The inspected full-page capture showed huge blank gaps and an overloaded hero, so those parts were not carried forward.

### 2. Outsourced.ph

URL: https://outsourced.ph/

Outsourced.ph pairs a dark navy hero with cyan illustration, proof cards, a four-step hiring explanation, office photography, and a broad role catalog. The visual system is clear and friendly. The page repeats several sales actions and the cookie panel covered much of the hero in the inspected view.

Screenshot: `/home/hermes/.hermes/cache/screenshots/browser_screenshot_84286a225eaf49f2aedb3f44a79480de.png`

### 3. Booth

URL: https://hirebooth.com/

Booth uses deep green, lilac, rounded cards, candid workplace photography, large geometric type, and hand-drawn marks. The service cards and operating-model tabs make a complicated offer easier to scan. The inspected page had a large promotional overlay, empty sections, and counters stuck at zero, so the stable palette and editorial confidence were more useful than its page mechanics.

Screenshot: `/home/hermes/.hermes/cache/screenshots/browser_screenshot_13b7c2a11e9449b19f19367cf356ed35.png`

### 4. Cloudstaff

URL: https://www.cloudstaff.com/

Cloudstaff leads with branded employee portraits, technology cues, compliance claims, and a tidy role grid. Its three-step onboarding section is one of the clearest process explanations in the group. The page is very long, uses too many sales labels, and several large areas appeared empty in the inspected capture.

Screenshot: `/home/hermes/.hermes/cache/screenshots/browser_screenshot_a65fae8cc2004082a87739ba142aafc6.png`

### 5. Remote Staff

URL: https://www.remotestaff.com/

Remote Staff combines evergreen and lime with editorial serif headings, office photography, process cards, and a detailed savings calculator. The serif and sans-serif pairing feels more considered than a standard SaaS layout. Most of the inspected capture suffered from low-opacity text and excessive blank space, and the calculator does not fit this site's no-pricing rule.

## Selected direction

Winner: Sourcefit.

Sourcefit had the most useful design logic for this broad outsourcing-company niche: a bold promise, a visible operational artifact, and a direct connection between hiring and repeatable process. The new homepage turns that logic into an original "operations brief" rather than copying its orange palette, facility video, accordions, claims, or layout.

The final direction uses an ink-green and paper palette, brick-orange actions, editorial serif headlines, numbered function files, and a large locally stored meeting photograph with an original handoff-sheet overlay. The sections read like a working operations dossier: choose a function, draw the approval line, set the first review, then request a brief.

## Originality and policy boundaries

- The composition, copy, logo, cards, workflow sheet, palette, and CSS are original.
- No competitor copy, screenshots, logos, or other assets are used by the site.
- The public offer is limited to Filipino talent recruited and hired in the Philippines.
- Competitor references to other regions belong only in this private research note and do not describe this site's offer.
- No public rates, savings claims, customer logos, testimonials, ratings, awards, or invented performance figures were added.
- OutsourcedCompany.com is an independent lead-generation and information site. Copy near the main conversion points explains that requests may be routed to a Philippines-focused staffing partner.

The image was inspected before use. It visibly shows two people with laptops talking across a table in a glass-walled office. Reflections make it feel documentary rather than staged. The alt text describes only the visible scene. It does not claim that the people are Filipino, that they work for this site, or that the meeting took place in the Philippines. Nearby copy carries the Philippines-only staffing promise.

The photo works with an original on-image handoff sheet that supplies the operational cue. Desktop QA must keep the central laptop, gesturing hand, and sheet legible without covering one another.

## Logo

`public/logo.svg` is an original operations-folder mark. The folded page, two check lines, and circular seal echo the homepage brief without borrowing a competitor's symbol.
